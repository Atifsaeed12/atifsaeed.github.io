About

I am a Lecturer in Computer Science at FAST National University of Computer and Emerging Sciences (FAST-NUCES), Islamabad, and a Ph.D. researcher in Computer Science. My research focuses on developing trustworthy and adaptive artificial intelligence systems that can learn continuously from dynamic environments while maintaining robustness, explainability, and privacy.

My primary research interests span Machine Learning, Deep Learning, Continual Learning, Federated Learning and Unlearning, Trustworthy AI, Large Language Models, Financial Time-Series Forecasting, Medical AI, and Computer Vision. My current doctoral research investigates continual learning frameworks for non-stationary financial markets, integrating adaptive neural architectures with temporal knowledge retention to improve long-term stock market forecasting performance.

Beyond financial AI, I work on privacy-preserving federated learning, explainable medical image analysis, multimodal intelligence, retrieval-augmented generation (RAG), and trustworthy LLM systems. My goal is to build intelligent systems that remain reliable under distribution shifts while providing interpretable and reproducible decision-making.

I have authored and co-authored research articles, book chapters, and conference papers in artificial intelligence and data science, appearing in international venues covering machine learning, healthcare AI, recommender systems, federated learning, and financial analytics. I actively collaborate with researchers and supervise undergraduate and postgraduate research projects in these areas.

Before joining FAST-NUCES, I served as a Lecturer at SZABIST Islamabad and as an Associate Lecturer at Capital University of Science and Technology (CUST), teaching undergraduate and graduate courses in software engineering, machine learning, programming, and data science.

Research

My research aims to build intelligent systems capable of learning continuously from dynamic data while remaining robust, interpretable, and privacy-preserving — spanning methodological ML research and domain-specific applications in finance, healthcare, and intelligent decision support.

Continual learning for financial forecasting

Adaptive deep learning models for non-stationary stock market prediction using continual learning and temporal knowledge retention.

Federated learning and unlearning

Privacy-preserving distributed learning with certified machine unlearning, explainability, and robustness against heterogeneous client distributions.

Trustworthy large language models

Hallucination detection, citation verification, retrieval-augmented generation, and reliable inference for LLM-based systems.

Medical artificial intelligence

Deep learning, explainable AI, and federated learning methods for disease diagnosis using medical imaging and clinical data.

Recommendation systems

Intelligent recommendation models using graph neural networks, attention mechanisms, and multimodal representation learning.

Doctoral research

Continual learning frameworks for stock market prediction that adapt to changing market regimes while mitigating catastrophic forgetting through efficient memory and knowledge retention.
